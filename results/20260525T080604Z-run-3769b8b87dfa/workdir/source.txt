[P1] The annual butterfly census at Marsh Reserve recorded 412 species
in 2023, down from 437 in 2022.

[P2] Researchers attribute the decline primarily to an unusually dry
spring, which reduced nectar availability for adult butterflies.

[P3] Secondary factors include increased nighttime light pollution
near the eastern boundary of the reserve and a localized outbreak of
a parasitic wasp affecting the genus Vanessa.

[P4] The reserve management has scheduled a habitat restoration
initiative for spring 2026, focused on wildflower planting and
selective lighting reduction.
