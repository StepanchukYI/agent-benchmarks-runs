When water droplets in the atmosphere interact with beams, Snell's law describes how the beams bend at the air-water interface due to the refractive index difference. The droplets act as spheres that reflect internally before exiting, creating a circular arc that appears to observers correctly. This optical phenomenon becomes visible when viewing from a specific cone of view angle relative to the source. Each droplet contributes a portion to the overall array. Multiple droplets form the resulting ribbon.
