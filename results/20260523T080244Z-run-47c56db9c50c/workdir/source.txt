The following three quotes appear in the same archive volume,
labelled by their respective speakers. Note that the quotes are
intentionally similar but NOT identical.

Speaker A, the cartographer Adis: "The road north of Eldermere
turns west at the cairn marked by three stacked stones."

Speaker B, the surveyor Roen: "The road north of Eldermere turns
west at the cairn marked with three stacked stones."

Speaker C, the historian Lyra: "The road north of Eldermere bends
west at the cairn marked by three stacked stones."

(Note for the reader: the prepositions and verbs differ — `marked
by`, `marked with`, `marked by`; `turns`, `turns`, `bends`. The
attribution distinguishes them.)
